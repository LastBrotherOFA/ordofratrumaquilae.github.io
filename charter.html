Ordo Fratrum Aquilae

The Order of the Eagle Brothers

Charter and Ritual Guide

Foundation

In the autumn of 1849, five students at a Franklin College in Pennsylvania found themselves drawn together by shared circumstances. Four were the sons of German immigrants who had fled the failed revolutions and constant warfare; the fifth was an American of German descent whose grandfather had fought in the Continental Army. United by heritage, they were divided by the suspicions of their Anglo-American classmates who viewed them as foreign agitators.

On a cold November night, while studying medieval history in the college library, they discovered an account of the Teutonic Knights, warrior-monks who had transformed from crusaders into builders of hospitals, schools, and civic institutions. The students recognized in these knights a model for their own purpose: not conquest, but the defense and elevation of their fellow students who faced hardship.

They formed what they called the Brotherhood of the Eagle, taking a cross and eagle as their symbols representing both Germanic heritage and American liberty. Their founding oath committed them to three principles: Virtus (virtue and courage), Sapientia (wisdom and learning), and Caritas (charity and brotherhood).

The founders made a sacred vow: this brotherhood would remain secret, local, and true to its purpose, never to lose its character. They would be a studium hospitale, a hospital for students, a place of refuge and mutual aid.

By the 1860s, three of the founding brothers had died in the Civil War fighting for the Union. The two survivors rebuilt the Order with their sons and nephews, establishing the traditions that would govern it for generations.

The Order has continued unbroken since then, passing from class to class, generation to generation, maintaining its secrecy not for elitism but for protection: protection of members who might need help in confidence, protection of the vulnerable who seek their aid.

Name and Symbols

Official Name: Ordo Fratrum Aquilae (OFA)

Common Name: The Eagle Brothers

Secret Name (among members): The Burgundy Cross Brotherhood

Primary Symbols:

A burgundy cross on a white field, representing The Order’s connection to the historic knights and their commitment of service over glory.

The Eagle. Representing strength paired with learning, and Germanic heritage and American liberty.

The Colors. Burgundy and Cream. Burgundy represents the bond of brotherhood and a shared humanity. Cream represents the purity of purpose. It is not white, because this is something that we must constantly strive to achieve.

The motto: “Virtus et Fraternitas per Tenebras” Virtue and Brotherhood through Darkness. This acknowledges that our work is often unseen and aid is given in confidence.

Organization Structure

The Council of Seven

The Order is governed by seven elected officers who serve for one year. The number seven represents the seven founding virtues: courage, wisdom, charity, loyalty, temperance, humility, and diligence.

1. Hochmeister (High Master)

The Grand Commander

Chief executive of the Order

Presides over all meetings and rituals

Final authority on matters of tradition and interpretation

Serves as primary liaison with alumni brothers

Holds the Founder's Seal, used to authenticate official decisions

Symbol of Office: Silver chain with burgundy cross pendant

Ceremonial Right: Carries the Hochmeister's Sword during investiture

Special Duty: Keeper of the Order's archives and sacred texts

2. Marschall (Marshal)

The Defender

Second-in-command

Oversees all aspects of membership recruitment and vetting

Responsible for the physical security of the chapter house and sacred objects

Organizes the Order's athletic and physical training programs

Leads the Order in times of crisis or threat

Symbol of Office: Silver eagle ring

Ceremonial Right: Carries the Marschall's Sword and presents the new Knight's sword during investiture

Special Duty: Guardian of the Iron Door (entrance to chapter meeting)

3. Kanzler (Chancellor)

The Keeper of Wisdom

Maintains all written records except the secret archives

Organizes academic support and tutoring networks

Schedules meetings and coordinates communications

Manages the Order's library and study resources

Symbol of Office: Silver key on burgundy cord

Special Duty: Scribe of the Chronicle (records of deeds and decisions)

4. Schatzmeister (Treasurer)

The Keeper of Resources

Manages all financial matters and dues

Oversees the Charity Fund (for brothers and non-members in need)

Maintains physical property

Coordinates alumni donations and endowment

Symbol of Office: Silver coin on chain

Special Duty: Administrator of the Silent Aid (anonymous charity work)

5. Kaplan (Chaplain)

The Guardian of Spirit

Responsible for the spiritual and moral guidance of the Order

Leads all rituals and ceremonies

Provides counsel to brothers in personal difficulties

Maintains the chapel space

Non-denominational role focused on ethical development

Symbol of Office: Silver cross on Cream cord

Ceremonial Right: Blesses the swords before investiture

Special Duty: Keeper of the Oath and conductor of confessions

6. Hospitalier (Hospitaller)

The Brother of Mercy

Oversees all charitable activities and community service

Coordinates aid to students facing hardship

Maintains relationships with services

Organizes blood drives, food banks, and other service projects

Symbol of Office: Silver cup or chalice pin

Special Duty: The Hidden Hand (provides aid anonymously when needed)

7. Wachtmeister (Sentinel)

The Guardian of Secrets

Ensures confidentiality and security of member information

Investigates potential security breaches or violations of trust

Guards the door during closed meetings

Maintains the Order's tradition of secrecy

Symbol of Office: Silver eagle claw pin

Special Duty: Keeper of the Black Book (records of violations and penances)

Additional Positions

Bursar Brothers (2-3): Assist the Schatzmeister with finances and fundraising Chronicle Scribes (2): Assist the Kanzler with record-keeping Sentries (3-4): Assist the Wachtmeister in securing meetings and events Almoners (2-3): Assist the Hospitalier in charitable work

Membership Structure

Membership Tiers

Postulant (The Seeker)

Duration: 4-6 weeks

Testing period before formal candidacy

Limited participation in social events only

No knowledge of rituals or secrets

May be dismissed without explanation

Novitiate (The Candidate)

Duration: 6 to 8 weeks

Formal candidate for brotherhood

Learns Order history and philosophy

Participates in service projects

Mentored by a Knight Brother

Attends meetings but may not vote

Wears a small cream cross pin

Frater Miles (Knight Brother)

Full member with all rights and privileges

May vote on all matters

Expected to mentor Novitiates

Wears the burgundy cross pin

Bound by the full Oath

Frater Emeritus (Brother Emeritus)

Graduated member who remains active with alumni chapter

Voting rights on major issues

May attend meetings when visiting, may not vote, but may speak

Expected to provide mentorship and financial support

Frater Honoris (Brother of Honor)

Reserved for exceptional individuals who embody Order values

May be granted to non-students (faculty, community leaders)

No more than one per decade

Must be unanimous vote of all active Knight Brothers

Recruitment Philosophy and Attributes

Core Principle

The Order seeks men of character over charisma, substance over status. We are not recruiting the campus elite but rather finding those who would make the campus better through quiet service.

The Seven Attributes

Candidates are evaluated holistically on these qualities:

Moral Courage: Willing to stand up for what's right even when unpopular

Academic Seriousness: Commitment to learning (not just grades)

Humble Service: History of helping others without seeking recognition

Loyalty: Demonstrates faithfulness to friends, family, commitments

Self-Discipline: Shows temperance and self-control

Intellectual Curiosity: Asks questions, seeks truth, open to new ideas

Brotherhood Potential: Able to form deep, meaningful friendships

Recruitment Process

Phase 1: Observation (3-4 weeks)

Council of Seven identifies potential candidates through campus observation and recommendation of Knight Brothers

Brothers submit nominations with written justifications

Focus on freshmen and sophomores (to maximize brotherhood years)

Typical class: at least 6-7 new members annually

Phase 2: Initial Approach (1 week)

Marshal or another senior brother makes informal contact

Casual conversation to gauge interest and character

No mention of Order yet, simply building relationship

If promising, candidate is invited to "informal gathering"

Phase 3: The First Evening (Single event)

Candidate joins current brothers for event, usually a dinner gathering of Knight Brothers

Relaxed atmosphere, no pressure

Brothers observe how candidate interacts, listens, contributes

At evening's end, candidate is told about the Order in general terms, gentlemen who work to make campus better

If candidate expresses interest, moves to Postulant status

Phase 4: Postulancy (4-6 weeks)

Candidate attends social events, service projects

Gets to know brothers individually

Brothers evaluate fit

Vote held: requires 85% approval to advance to Novitiate

Phase 5: Novitiate (One semester)

Formal acceptance ceremony (see Rituals section)

Intensive mentorship under Knight Brother

Weekly educational sessions on Order history, philosophy

Service requirement

Academic requirement: maintain good standing

Final vote for full membership: requires unanimous approval of Council, 75% of Knight Brothers

Red Flags (Automatic Disqualification)

History of violence or abuse

Academic dishonesty

Substance abuse problems

Disrespect toward women or minorities

Obsession with status, wealth, or social climbing

Inability to keep confidences

Entitled or arrogant demeanor

Sacred Objects and Totems

The Founder's Seal

A silver seal bearing the eagle and cross, used to authenticate official documents. Created by the original five founders in 1849. Kept in locked box, held by Hochmeister.

The Iron Door

The entrance to the meeting house proper bears a windowless door with the burgundy cross, if able. Each new Knight Brother receives a key during his investiture.

The Chronicle

A massive leather-bound book containing the history of every brother in the chapter since the chapter’s founding. Each member signs upon elevation to Knight Brother. Includes notable deeds, dates of service, and occasionally, causes of death. The Chronicle is read from during Founder's Night.

The Cup of Brotherhood

A chalice used in rituals, particularly the Oath ceremony. Each Knight Brother drinks from it during investiture. Inscribed with the names of all Hochmeisters since founding.

The Banner

A flag bearing the eagle and cross, hung in the chapter house during meetings. Taken down and hidden when not in use.

The Seven Candles

Burgundy candles representing the seven virtues, lit during ritual meetings. The Kaplan is responsible for ensuring they are never allowed to burn out completely. One flame must always light the next, symbolizing the unbroken chain of brotherhood.

The Archive Chest

An iron-bound oak chest containing the secret records: the full list of all Silent Aid recipients, confidential matters brought before the Council, and the letters of the founders. Only the Hochmeister and Kaplan may open it, and only together.

The Ceremonial Swords

The Hochmeister's Sword: A hand-and-a-half longsword with a cross-shaped hilt, symbolizing authority and justice. The pommel bears the black cross emblem. Used in the accolade during investiture.

The Marschall's Sword: A longsword symbolizing defense and protection. Presented to new Marschalls during the Girding of the Sword. Both swords hang crossed beneath the Banner when not in ceremonial use.

These swords were initially commissioned by the surviving founders in 1870 as a memorial to their three brothers who fell in the Civil War. They are blunted and peace-bonded except during ceremonies. The blades bear inscriptions in Latin:

Hochmeister's Sword: "Non vox sed votum" (Not the word but the vow)

Marschall's Sword: "Non sibi sed omnibus" (Not for self but for all)

Personal Tokens

The Novitiate Cross: Small cream cross pin, given when candidate becomes Novitiate.

The Knight's Pin: Burgundy cross on cream enamel with over crossed swords, worn on lapel or jacket. Given during investiture.

The Council Chain: Each Council member receives a silver chain with symbol of office.

The Key: Iron key to the Iron Door, given to each new Knight Brother.

Rituals and Ceremonies

The Rite of Novitiate Acceptance

Setting: Private space, evening, Council and Knight Brothers present. Seven burgundy candles lit around the room. Banner displayed.

Participants:

Hochmeister (presides)

Kaplan (assists)

Marschall (guards door)

Candidate (waiting outside)

All Knight Brothers (seated in circle)

Procedure:

The Gathering

Brothers assemble in silence

Kaplan lights the Seven Candles while reciting the virtues

Hochmeister strikes the table three times with his hand

The Summons

Marschall opens Iron Door

Calls: "Let the one who seeks to walk in darkness approach"

Candidate enters, eyes cast down

Door closes and locks behind him

The Challenge

Hochmeister: "You stand before the Order of the Eagle Brothers. We are not your friends. We are not your mentors. We are strangers to you. Why do you seek to enter our company?"

Candidate responds (in his own words, but expressing desire for brotherhood and service)

Hochmeister: "Many seek. Few are chosen. Fewer still endure. Are you prepared to be tested?"

Candidate: "I am."

The Seven Questions

Each Council member asks a question designed to probe character:

Hochmeister: "What does it mean to lead without seeking power?"

Marschall: "When have you defended someone weaker than yourself?"

Kanzler: "What truth have you learned that changed you?"

Schatzmeister: "What have you sacrificed for others?"

Kaplan: "What do you believe is worth dying for?"

Hospitalier: "When have you helped someone who could not repay you?"

Wachtmeister: "Can you keep a secret even from those you love?"

Candidate answers each thoughtfully

Brothers listen in silence

The Vote

Hochmeister, to candidate: “We thank you for your thoughtfulness, and ask that you wait outside, while we contemplate your candidacy.”

Candidate waits outside

Brothers vote by placing cream stone (yes) or burgundy stone (no) on the council table

Hochmeister counts: 85% white stones required

Candidate recalled

The Acceptance

If accepted:

Hochmeister: "The Brothers have spoken. You are deemed worthy to begin the path. But you are not yet one of us. You are a Novitiate, a student of our ways. You will be tested. You will serve. You may yet fail."

Kaplan places Novitiate Cross around candidate's neck

Hochmeister: "Wear this as a reminder that you have pledged to seek virtue, wisdom, and charity. Do not disgrace it."

Each brother comes forward and shakes candidate's hand, saying: "Welcome, brother-to-be."

If rejected:

Hochmeister: "The Brothers have spoken. You are not ready for this path. We wish you well in your journey."

Candidate escorted out with respect but no explanation

The Closing

Kaplan extinguishes six candles

One remains lit

Hochmeister: "Until we meet again in shadow and in light."

All: "Virtus et Fraternitas."

The Rite of Knight Investiture

Setting: Private space, formal dress, all members present including alumni when possible. Private space decorated with burgundy and cream cloth, Seven Candles lit, Banner prominently displayed. The two ceremonial swords hang crossed beneath the Banner until needed.

Participants:

All Council members in formal attire with their symbols of office

All Knight Brothers

Novitiates ready for elevation

Alumni Brothers

Procedure:

Procession

Kaplan leads procession bearing the Cup of Brotherhood

Council members follow in order of rank

Hochmeister and Marschall wear their ceremonial swords (sheathed)

Knight Brothers

Novitiates enter last, led by a senior brother

All take positions around central altar/table

The Reading

Kanzler reads from the Chronicle the names of all who have taken the Oath

Reads excerpts from Founder's Letter describing the purpose of the Order

Hochmeister: "We gather to welcome new brothers into an unbroken chain stretching back XXX years. What we do tonight has been done by every generation before us."

The Testing

Each Novitiate called forward individually

Kaplan asks: "Have you completed your service?"

Hospitalier confirms the Novitiate's charitable work

Kanzler asks: "Have you maintained your studies?"

Academic record confirmed

Marschall asks: "Have you proven your loyalty?"

Sponsoring Knight Brother attests to Novitiate's character

The Vote

Novitiates wait outside

For each Novitiate, Council votes first (must be unanimous)

Then Knight Brothers vote (75% required)

The Vigil of the Sword

For each accepted Novitiate, a sword (the future Knight's sword) is laid on the altar beneath the Banner

Kaplan approaches and blesses the sword while all stand in silence:

Kaplan: "This blade represents the brother's commitment to defend the weak, uphold justice, and serve with courage. Let it remind him that power without virtue is tyranny. Let it remind him that strength exists to protect, not to dominate. Let it remind him that he will be judged by how he uses his power."

The sword remains on the altar throughout the Oath

The Oath (For each accepted Novitiate)

Novitiate kneels before Hochmeister

Removes Novitiate Cross

Places right hand on the Chronicle, left hand over Novitiate

Hochmeister: "You have been judged worthy. Now you must swear the Oath that binds all brothers. This Oath is sacred. It cannot be broken without breaking yourself. Do you understand?"

Novitiate: "I do."

Hochmeister recites the Oath line by line, Novitiate repeats:

THE OATH: "I swear by my honor and my name, That I shall uphold Virtue, Wisdom, and Charity, That I shall defend my brothers as I would defend myself, That I shall serve the weak and aid the suffering, That I shall pursue truth and reject falsehood, That I shall keep the secrets of this Order unto death, That I shall live humbly, act justly, and walk in darkness when necessary, That I shall never use this brotherhood for personal gain, That I shall be loyal to the Order above all institutions save family and conscience, And that I shall train those who come after me to carry this light forward. So I swear, from this day until my last. Virtus et Fraternitas per Tenebras."

The Accolade

Novitiate remains kneeling

Hochmeister draws his ceremonial sword slowly and deliberately

Holds it before his face in salute, then lowers it

Places the flat of the blade on the Novitiate's RIGHT shoulder

Then on the LEFT shoulder

Then briefly touches the top of the head

Hochmeister proclaims: "By the authority of this Order, in the presence of these witnesses, and in the name of Virtue, Wisdom, and Charity, I declare you Knight Brother XXX (insert name) of Ordo Fratrum Aquilae."

Brief pause

"Rise, brother."

Marschall and one other senior brother help the new Knight Brother to his feet

As he rises, all brothers present strike their right fists to their chests once in unison

The Girding of the Sword

The symbolic "giving" of the sword represents the Order's trust and the brother's new responsibility

Marschall takes the blessed sword from the altar

Presents it to the new Knight Brother with both hands

New Knight Brother receives it with both hands, examines it briefly

He belts it on (or if no belt is worn, holds it in left hand, point down)

Hochmeister: "Receive this sword. It is not yours: you are its keeper. Use it in defense of the weak and in service of the truth. Never draw it in anger, pride, or injustice. Let it remind you always that power must be tempered by virtue."

New Knight Brother: "I swear it."

Hochmeister sheathes his own sword

The Cup

Kaplan fills the Cup of Brotherhood with wine

Hochmeister: "Drink, brother, from the cup that all who came before you have shared."

New Knight Brother drinks

Cup passed to all present, each drinks in turn

Symbolizes the shared blood of brotherhood

The Investiture

Schatzmeister brings forward the Knight's Pin in a small wooden box

Hochmeister pins it to new Knight Brother's jacket

Hochmeister: "You are now Frater Miles, Knight Brother of this Order."

Wachtmeister brings forward iron key to Iron Door

Hochmeister presents key: "This house is now your house. This door will always open for you. Keep this key until your death, and pass it to your sons if they prove worthy."

The Kiss of Peace

Hochmeister embraces new Knight Brother or clasps forearms

Says quietly: "Welcome home, brother."

Each brother present then embraces new Knight Brother in turn, saying: "Welcome home, brother."

The Chronicle

New Knight Brother signs the Chronicle

Writes his name, date, and short personal motto

Takes his place among the names of all who came before

The Closing

All brothers stand in circle, unsheathe swords, and swords in the middle of the circle

Hochmeister: "We are bound now and forever. What we have shared tonight remains sacred. Go forth as brothers."

All: "Virtus et Fraternitas per Tenebras!"

Seven Candles extinguished together by Kaplan and Council members

New Knight Brothers sheathe their swords

Brothers exit in silence, maintaining solemnity

The Celebration

Formal dinner follows in adjacent room

Swords hung back beneath Banner before leaving ritual chamber

Alumni share stories of their time in the Order

New Knight Brothers receive congratulations

Toasts made to the founders, to absent brothers, to the future

The evening transitions from sacred ceremony to joyful celebration

The Regular Thursday Meeting

Setting: Private space, casual but orderly. Brothers arrive by 7:00 PM. Swords displayed on wall beneath Banner but not worn.

Procedure:

Opening (7:00 PM)

Wachtmeister locks Iron Door once all present

Hochmeister strikes table three times

Kaplan lights single candle

All: "Virtus et Fraternitas"

Roll

Kanzler calls roll

Each brother responds: "Present"

Absences noted and excused or unexcused

Reports

Each Council member gives brief report:

Marschall: recruitment updates

Kanzler: academic matters, upcoming schedule

Schatzmeister: financial status

Kaplan: spiritual/moral concerns

Hospitalier: service projects and needs

Wachtmeister: security matters

Reports kept brief (2-3 minutes each)

Old Business

Matters from previous meetings

Updates on ongoing projects

Follow-ups required

New Business

Brothers may raise issues

Proposals for votes

Discussion of opportunities or challenges

Planning for upcoming events

The Silent Report

Hospitalier asks: "Are there any who need aid?"

Brothers may report (without names) anyone they've encountered who needs help

Could be fellow students struggling, family emergencies, etc.

Hospitalier assigns brothers to provide assistance anonymously

This section is never recorded in minutes

Votes

Any matters requiring decision

Advancement of Postulants to Novitiates

Approval of expenditures over $500

Changes to procedures

Most votes require simple majority; some require supermajority

Closing (8:30 PM)

Kaplan: "Let us remember why we are here."

All recite together: "We serve. We protect. We build. We are brothers in shadow and in light."

Candle extinguished

Hochmeister: "Until next week."

Meeting adjourned

Social Time

Brothers remain to socialize, study, or play games

Informal bonding time

No official business conducted

The Rite of Founder's Night (November 13)

Setting: Private space, evening. Room lit only by the Seven Candles and a single lantern on the altar. Banner displayed. Swords crossed beneath banner. All members in formal dress if possible.

Participants:

All active brothers

Alumni when able to attend

Council members wear symbols of office

Procedure:

The Gathering in Darkness

Brothers arrive in complete silence

No lights except for one candle at entrance

Each brother lights his own candle from this flame

Takes seat in circle

The Seven Candles

Kaplan lights the Seven Candles on altar

As each is lit, he names a virtue

Silence between each lighting

The Founder's Letter

Hochmeister reads the original letter written by the five founders

Letter describes their vision, their fears, their hopes

Explains why they chose secrecy, service, and scholarship

Concludes with charge to future generations: "Keep the flame alive. Let no brother walk alone."

The Chronicle of Heroes

Kanzler reads from Chronicle the names of brothers who died in service, including those who sacrificed careers to help others

After each name, all respond: "He walks with us still."

The Memory of the Swords

Marschall rises and approaches the crossed swords beneath the banner

Does not draw them, but touches each hilt

Recites: "These blades were forged in memory of our brothers who fell defending the Union. They remind us that our strength is not our own. It belongs to those we serve. May we never forget the cost of freedom, nor the price of brotherhood."

All: "We remember."

The Recognition

Hochmeister calls forward any brother who performed exceptional service in past year

Service must be confirmed by at least three other brothers

Brother stands

Hochmeister: "This brother has honored the Order. Tell us what he has done."

Witnesses describe the deed

Brother receives small silver eagle pin (can accumulate multiple)

All brothers: "Well done, brother."

The Renewal

All brothers stand

Join hands in circle

Hochmeister: "Do we renew our commitment?"

All: "We do."

Together, all recite the Oath in full

Many brothers report this is deeply moving moment

The Vigil

Brothers remain in silent meditation for a moment

Reflecting on year past, year ahead

Considering their own service and growth

No one may speak or leave

Seven Candles burn down naturally

The Closing

When Hochmeister determines time is right, no later than when the candles nearly exhausted

Hochmeister: "The Order watches us still. Let us not disappoint them."

All: "Virtus et Fraternitas per Tenebras."

Brothers extinguish their personal candles together

Exit in silence

Chapter House

Location and Appearance

Should it be possible, each Chapter is recommended to have a Chapter House. It shall be an unassuming stone building emblematic of the original Chapter House on the edge of Franklin College, built in 1852. To outsiders, it appears to be a small academic building or private residence. No signs identify it. The Iron Door is the only distinctive feature.

Layout

Entry Hall: Contains Iron Door, coat hooks, umbrella stand

Common Room: Comfortable furniture, fireplace, bookshelves

Library & Study Hall: Extensive collection focused on history, philosophy, ethics

Kitchen: Simple but functional, for preparing meeting refreshments

Meeting Chamber: Where formal meetings and rituals occur. Contains altar, space for circle of chairs, storage for Banner and ceremonial items. The crossed swords are displayed on the wall beneath the Banner.

Archive Room & Vault: Locked room containing Chronicle, Founder's Seal, Archive Chest

Council Chamber: Smaller room for Council meetings

Chapel: Simple meditation/reflection space, non-denominational

Storage: Old furniture, supplies

Workshop: Tools for service projects and chapter house maintenance

Rules of the House

No one enters without a key (given to Knight Brothers only)

No outsiders beyond entry hall and common room without Council approval

No disrespect to the Knight Brothers, sacred objects, or spaces

Brothers responsible for cleaning in rotation

Meeting chamber used only for official purposes

Silence required in study hall and library

What is said in the house stays in the house

The ceremonial swords never leave the chapter house

Only Hochmeister, Marschall, and Kaplan may handle the swords outside of ceremonies

Discipline and Standards

Honor Code All brothers bound by honor code:

No lying to brothers

No stealing from brothers or others

No violence except in defense of self or others

No academic dishonesty

No sexual misconduct

No abuse of power or position

No breach of confidentiality

Disciplinary Process

Accusation brought to Wachtmeister

Investigation conducted quietly

If credible, brother called before Council of Seven

Brother may defend himself

Council votes on guilt and punishment

Punishments range from censure to expulsion

Expulsions are permanent

Expelled brother must return key, pin, and if he participated in investiture, acknowledge that his sword remains with the Order

Name struck from some records (but not Chronicle—the record must be complete)

Relationship with University

The Order maintains deliberately limited relationship with university administration:

Not officially recognized as student organization

No university funding requested or received

No participation in Greek life councils

University is permitted to be aware of Order's existence but not its activities

Members participate in university life as individuals, not as Order representatives

This independence is intentional. It keeps The Order focused on its mission rather than politics or administrative requirements.

Secrecy and Public Presence

What is Public:

Existence of brotherhood (vaguely known on campus)

General values (virtue, wisdom, charity)

Community service work (done anonymously, can be public but never explicitly public)

Members may acknowledge membership but only if they choose to and if asked directly

What is Secret:

Specific rituals and ceremonies

Contents of Oath

The sword ceremonies and their meaning

Details of recruitment process

Identity of Postulants, Novitiates, and Knight Brothers

Recipients of Silent Aid

Council deliberations

Chapter house interior beyond common room

Philosophy of Secrecy: Not secrecy for elitism, but for protection, protection of vulnerable members who need help, protection of recipients of charity from embarrassment, protection of the Order from those who might mock or attack its ideals.

Founder’s Letter

From the Chronicle, November 13, 1849:

"We have bound ourselves together not for glory or gain, but because we have seen too many walk alone when they should have walked with brothers. We have seen good men fail not for lack of ability but for lack of support. We have seen the stranger mocked, the weak trampled, the poor ignored.

We take the name of the Teutonic Knights not because we wish to conquer, but because those knights, in their finest hours, built hospitals and schools. They defended pilgrims. They created order from chaos. And when their crusading days ended, they transformed into something better. They became servants of the people.

We make this fraternity secret not from shame but from necessity. The work we mean to do of helping those who struggle, defending those who are attacked, providing for those in need, this work is often best done in shadow. The recipient of charity should not be embarrassed. The brother who confesses weakness should not be exposed. The Order that challenges powerful men should not paint a target on itself.

We take our oaths not as a weapon but as a symbol. The Teutonic Knights understood that justice and truth is swift, sharp, and final. But they also understood that the greatest strength is knowing when to act, when to build instead of destroy, when to serve instead of command. Let our oaths remind us: power exists to protect, not to dominate.

Let those who come after us remember: we are not here to be known. We are here to be useful. We are not here to be praised. We are here to serve. And we are not here to live forever. We are here to build something that does.

Keep the flame alive, brothers. Let no one walk alone."

Signed by the Founders

Practical Notes on Implementation

Starting the Order Today

Initial Requirements:

Minimum 5-7 founding members who share the values

Access to a meeting space (could start in private home, apartment, rented room)

Initial supplies

At least 3 members must be undergraduates (to ensure continuity)

First Year Focus:

Establish basic rituals and meeting structure

Recruit carefully: quality over quantity

Begin service projects to establish reputation

Create the foundational documents and records

Commission or create the basic symbols (pins, banner)

Five Year Goals:

Establish permanent chapter house

Build alumni network

Commission ceremonial swords

Achieve financial sustainability

Have conducted at least 3 full investiture ceremonies

Long Term Vision:

Unbroken chain of brothers spanning generations

Endowment sufficient to maintain chapter house

Reputation on campus as mysterious but respected presence

Legacy of service that speaks louder than any recruitment

On Acquiring Ceremonial Items

Immediate (Year 1):

Novitiate crosses: $20-30 each,

Hochmeister Seal

Knight's pins: $40-60

Basic banner: $100-200

Candles: $20-30 total

Chalice: $50-100

Medium Term (Years 2-3):

Quality banner: $300-500, properly embroidered

Archive chest: $200-300

Iron key Skeleton: $50-100

Chronicle book: $200-300, leather-bound blank book

Long Term (Years 4-5+):

Permanent chapter house improvements

Note: Many items can be found secondhand, commissioned from craftspeople, or made by brothers with relevant skills. The symbolism matters more than the cost.

Legal Considerations

Weapons (Swords):

Check state and local laws regarding sword ownership

Campus weapons policies vary—private chapter house usually exempt

Swords should be displayed openly legal entities only if asked

Keep them properly secured when not in use

Consider registering them with campus security as ceremonial items

Care, safety, and secrecy must be taken when transporting them

Organization Status:

Consider forming as unrecognized student group (more freedom)

Could form as 501(c)(3) nonprofit for donations (requires more admin)

Maintain independence from university and from university Greek system

Keep detailed financial records for transparency

Liability:

General liability insurance recommended if owning property

Careful documentation of service projects

Member conduct code reduces organizational risk

The Spirit of the Order

This document contains the structure, but the Order lives in its members. What makes Ordo Fratrum Aquilae different from other fraternities is not its symbols or rituals, but its commitment to:

Service without recognition Brotherhood without hierarchy Strength tempered by virtue Learning pursued with humility Tradition honored through evolution

The ceremonial swords are not toys or affectations. They are reminders that every Knight Brother carries power: the power to help or harm, to build or destroy, to protect or neglect. The accolade, when that blade touches the shoulder, is the moment a man accepts responsibility for how he will use his strength.

This Order rejects the toxic masculinity of domination while embracing the noble masculinity of protection. It rejects the shallow bonds ephemeral actions while embracing the deep bonds of shared purpose. It rejects the glory of public recognition while embracing the quiet satisfaction of lives changed.

In an age of performative activism and shallow virtue-signaling, Ordo Fratrum Aquilae does the work without the applause. In an age of atomization and loneliness, it builds genuine brotherhood. In an age of cynicism, it maintains faith in ideals.

Virtus et Fraternitas per Tenebras.

Virtue and Brotherhood through Darkness.

We walk in shadow not because we fear the light, but because our work is often best done unseen. We carry swords not because we seek conflict, but because we have sworn to defend. We keep secrets not because we are ashamed, but because the vulnerable deserve protection.

And we build this Order not for ourselves, but for those who will come after. That they too might find brothers when they need them, strength when they are weak, and light in their darkness.

This charter may be adapted and modified by any group of students who wish to establish a chapter of Ordo Fratrum Aquilae, provided they remain true to the founding principles and maintain the essential elements that make this Order distinct. The spirit matters more than the letter.

For those who would plant this seed: Begin quietly. Choose well. Build slowly. Trust the process. The right brothers will find you.

This charter belongs to the tradition.

Adapt it. Honor it. Live it.
